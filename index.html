More Than a Retreat

This is about discovering your calling as a God-Bearer and living it boldly

What does it mean to live with Mary's heart? To hold nothing back from God, no matter what your life looks like?

"Let it be done unto me according to your word." -Luke 1:38

Join us for the 2026 Byzantine Catholic Young Adult Convocation, on July 31-August 2, where we will discover how her monastic heart can become ours.

Three Days of Transformation

Built around liturgical prayer, the heartbeat of monastic life

Our weekend will take place at the beginning of the Dormition Fast, when the whole Church is turning towards the Theotokos. Through liturgical prayer and talks from our speakers, we will learn to say with her "Let it be done unto me, according to your word".

Meet Your Speakers

Mother Natalia

Christ the Bridegroom Monastery, Burton OH

Mother Natalia entered Christ the Bridegroom Monastery in 2015, making her life profession in 2021. She co-hosts the What God is Not Podcast with Father Michael O'Loughlin, where she shares her joyful love of Christ in conversations about prayer, mystery, and the spiritual life.

Fr. Paul West

Our Lady of Perpetual Help, Levittown PA

Fr. Paul West was ordained a priest of the Byzantine Catholic Church in 2020 and currently serves Our Lady of Perpetual Help in Levittown, PA. Through his speaking and podcast work, he invites listeners to explore the spirituality of the Christian East and to cultivate lives of prayer rooted in the tradition of the Church.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is this convocation for?

This weekend is for young adults (generally ages 18–35) who desire deeper prayer, community, and a life offered fully to God, even if you are unsure how this looks or have questions.

Do I have to be Byzantine Catholic?

No. The convocation is rooted in the Byzantine Catholic tradition, but anyone of any faith background is welcome. As the Byzantine Catholic Church is in full communion with the Catholic churches, Roman Catholics also fulfill their Sunday Mass obligation by attending Divine Liturgy with us.

What about age limits?

The event is designed for young adults. If you are around the 18–35 range and are serious about growing in your faith, you are in the right place.

Is housing included?

Housing is not included in the registration fee, but there are several hotels within a short drive of the parish. Once you register, we will send suggested lodging options and, where possible, information about ride or room–sharing.

What if I cannot afford the full cost?

Please do not let cost be the reason you stay home. Need-based scholarships and assistance are available. There is a place on the registration form to request help, or you can email us directly with your situation.

What should I expect spiritually?

Expect a weekend centered on liturgical prayer, silence, and solid teaching, rather than a conference with constant noise. There will be plenty of time for fellowship, Q&A, and meeting other young adults.
